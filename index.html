About Me

Designing with Purpose

Human-Centered Design Philosophy

As an HCI designer, I believe that technology should serve people, not the other way around. My approach combines empathy, research, and aesthetics to create digital experiences that are both functional and delightful.

Currently pursuing my Master's in Human-Computer Interaction at Stanford University, I specialize in creating interfaces that improve accessibility, enhance user satisfaction, and solve real-world problems.

Methodology

My Design Process

A systematic approach to creating meaningful digital experiences

01

Discover

Conduct user research, stakeholder interviews, and competitive analysis to understand user needs and business goals.

  • User Interviews
  • Surveys
  • Personas
02

Define

Synthesize research findings to define problem statements, user journeys, and design requirements.

  • Journey Mapping
  • Problem Framing
  • Requirements
03

Design

Create wireframes, prototypes, and visual designs that address user needs and business objectives.

  • Wireframing
  • Prototyping
  • Visual Design
04

Test & Iterate

Conduct usability testing, gather feedback, and refine designs based on user insights and data.

  • Usability Testing
  • A/B Testing
  • Analytics
